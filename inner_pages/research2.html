Exploring the Effects of Generative AI Prompting Instruction on Undergraduate Students' Creative Problem Solving: From Learning to Using in a Classroom-Based Social Experiment

Smart Learning Environment (SCI Q1, Third Author, Under Review)

Juan Wu*, Jingwen Pan, Yaoyuan Zhou, Mengyu Liu, Ronghuai Huang

Abstract

TAs one of the core skills of the 21st-century, how to use generative artificial intelligence (GAI) to enhance creative problem solving (CPS) has become a key concern for educators. This study integrates GAI prompting instruction (PI) into the CPS process model and aims to explore the effects of PI on undergraduate students’ CPS and reveal how the prompting strategies used in student–GAI interactions dynamically influence the development of CPS ability. A classroom-based social experiment method was used to design CPS activities across three sessions of an undergraduate general education course, and two GAI PI interventions were implemented. The students’ GAI interaction logs, task outputs, and pre- and postintervention questionnaire data were collected. Through data processing methods, such as discourse analysis, quantitative statistical analysis, and process mining, the following conclusions are drawn: (1) GAI PI effectively enhances undergraduate students’ CPS ability; (2) PI efficiently expands the depth and breadth of students’ creative thinking processes; and (3) different types of prompting strategies have distinct effects on students’ CPS performance across various task stages. This study provides insights into the intrinsic mechanisms by which GAI affects the development of students’ CPS ability, offers theoretical support for teachers to foster students’ CPS ability using GAI PI, and serves as a reference for guiding students’ development path from learning to using GAI prompts.
